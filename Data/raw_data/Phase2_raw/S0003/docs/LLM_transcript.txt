Here’s the distinction clearly:

* An **invariant violation** usually means your system breaks a condition that was already defined and supposed to always hold.
* A **missing constraint** means the specification changed (or was incomplete), and your implementation does not yet enforce that new rule.

In your case:

* Your function was correct under the previous spec.
* A new business rule was introduced: “No meeting may start at or after 15:00 on Fridays.”
* Since your current code does not enforce that, it would now produce outputs that violate the updated requirements.

So the failure would most likely be classified as:

> The implementation does not enforce a newly introduced constraint.

Only after you accept the Friday rule as part of the formal specification does it become an invariant:

> For all returned slots on Fridays, start_time < 15:00.

Before that, it’s simply missing logic.

From a requirements engineering perspective (which your course likely cares about), this is:
